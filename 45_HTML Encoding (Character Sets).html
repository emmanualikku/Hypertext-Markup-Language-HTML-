HTML Encoding Example

This page uses UTF-8 encoding.

Here are some special characters:

  • Less than: <
  • Greater than: >
  • Ampersand: &
  • Quotation mark: "
  • Apostrophe: '
  • Non-breaking space:  
  • Copyright: ©
  • Registered trademark: ®
  • Euro sign: €
  • Yen sign: ¥
  • Pound sign: £
  • Cent sign: ¢
  • Degree sign: °
  • Plus-minus sign: ±
  • Multiplication sign: ×
  • Division sign: ÷
